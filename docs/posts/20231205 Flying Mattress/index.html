Flying Mattress

travel
Author

DK

Published

December 5, 2023

One way to find your influence or status in the family is to invite more people to your home than you have beds and bedrooms.

We had such an occasion during a family wedding.

Some visitors chose to go the Airbnb route or a traditional hotel stay.

Some, however, chose the more established means – staying with relatives.

Current Stock

That kicked off the planning.

Which visiting family gets which bedrooms and who gets to sleep on the floor versus beds.

I missed the cut on both and wound up sleeping on the floor with a mat.

The mat was about 30” by 72” (I measured since it looked kind of narrow).

The size of the mat and the material seemed familiar.

It was similar to a business class, lie flat, offerings for long distance flights.

Both in terms of construction material and the aforementioned dimensions.

Only missing items were the entertainment console with a private reading lamp and attendant call button.

Earlier Travels

There was a time when I would look forward to long flights. Because it meant travel in a sit/lie down on those beds in the sky.

My stock had fallen far and quickly.

This time around, I was a few inches off the ground. There was no need to worry about falling off. I slept as well as I did on my regular bed.

Probably because, unlike those trips abroad. I didn’t need the help of noise cancellation headphones or had the distraction of dozens of movies on demand.

And in some ways, sleeping on the floor is the traditional Korean way. The way I was raised for the first 10 years

Travel courtesy of corporations

Nearly all of my travels abroad and some within the United States were made possible due to my employment.

Worked for a company that was expanding geographically and employed a full time travel coordinator on-site.

The prevailing rule for long distance travel was if the travel was longer than 8 hours, everyone traveled in the Business Class.

The company was expecting me to best represent them and was kind enough to offer nice accommodations to overcome the inevitable jet-lag.

That fact should not make me any more or less than who I am

Perception of value

Like Ronins (浪人) of the past, when companies no longer expand, corporate travel warriors are no longer in demand.

One’s value may have changed or diminished.

Or the focus of the organization is no longer geographic expansion.

Those traveling days as well as any roles outside of the family fades and declines over time.

Now I am spending more time with my family.

What am I judged on?

Practical skills like cooking, cleaning, and tending to kids.

A greater responsibility, less recognition and far less reward – as far as things of this world’s measurement

Rewarded according to the skills in demand

Families may not demand certain skills or services right away. And in some cases, when one is not around, they will seek that service elsewhere

But you are evaluated constantly

In the end, individuals that comprise the family are far more critical.

They do not give out grades or salaries Something more equitable. They become what you spend time doing and building

Now I understand my role better. Also what is a timely role versus timeless role.

Sometimes one can get the 2 mixed up and expect one to be always available even after an extended time away.

Enticing and somewhat enjoyable were those times away from the family.

However, I am willing to take that mat on the floor at home over any fancy accommodations at a ground level or at 37,000 feet in the air.
