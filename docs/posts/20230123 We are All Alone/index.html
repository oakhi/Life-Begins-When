"When you’re happy, you enjoy the music but when you’re sad, you understand the lyrics"

― Frank Ocean

I have been contemplating lyrics lately.

Not necessarily because I am sad but because of my age and reflection on the days of our existence.

At a recent wedding, the couple danced to the music that accompanies these lines

I know your eyes in the morning sun

I feel you touch me in the pouring rain

And the moment that you wander far from me

I wanna feel you in my arms again

(How deep is your love?, Bee Gees 1977)

This was played during my high school days.


A corollary to Frank Ocean's statement might be,

When young, one dances to the music

When old, one contemplates memories invoked by lyrics.


During high school days, we owned a record album called “Silk Degrees”

It produced 4 singles and the album peaked at #2 on Billboard charts.

Back then, listened to who controlled the radio station playlist.

(Also there were no way of finding the official version of the lyrics. At times, it may be advantageous to leave the lyrics ambiguous. Used to think, learn not to pretent, instead of learn how to pretend)
